Jordan Swisher

The Office of the Provost engages in a broad range of initiatives designed to enhance GW’s academic stature. These include:

Signature Programs

Since 2002, The George Washington University has committed significant resources to enhancing academic programs with demonstrated strength in research and scholarship. In addition, we have invested in initiatives to strengthen the university writing program, language instruction, international initiatives, academic technology, and the Gelman Library’s electronic teaching resources. To date, the university has invested over $33 million in special funding to programs and initiatives. Read more.

Science and Engineering Hall

In October 2010, the GW Board of Trustees approved unanimously a plan to build a state-of-the-art Science and Engineering Hall on the Foggy Bottom campus. Faculty, student, and alumni input has been and will continue to be an integral part of the building planning process. Read more.

Medical Center Reorganization

The face of heath care is changing across the nation, and in May 2010, the Medical Center Committee of the George Washington University Board of Trustees asked GW's administration to conduct a review of the organizational structure of the GW Medical Center, in part to respond to this changing environment. Read more.
